<p>Six pieces of software I couldn’t live without this year.</p>
<p>1. <a title="http://www.zotero.org/" href="http://www.zotero.org/">Zotero</a>. I sung its praises before, and it is still managing my pdf’s (and cif files) to perfection.<br />
2. <a title="http://www.educ.umu.se/~cobian/cobianbackup.htm" href="http://www.educ.umu.se/%7Ecobian/cobianbackup.htm">Cobian.</a> Between daily backups to my USB key, to weekly backups to my external hardrive, Cobian does it all (<a title="http://homepage.mac.com/sweetcocoa/imsafe/" href="http://homepage.mac.com/sweetcocoa/imsafe/">Imsafe</a> fulfills the same needs, but on OS X).<br />
3. <a title="http://www.geocities.jp/kmo_mma/crystal/en/vesta.html" href="http://www.geocities.jp/kmo_mma/crystal/en/vesta.html">Vesta.</a> I owe the authors of Vesta a lot. Crystal structure <a title="../../2007/4/2_Render_this....html" href="http://web.mac.com/aronwalsh/notes_from_a_sceptical_alchymist/crystal_fields_%28start%29/Entries/2007/4/2_Render_this....html">visualization</a> and manipulation has never been so fast or beautiful. Make sure to cite their <a title="http://scripts.iucr.org/cgi-bin/paper?S0021889808012016" href="http://scripts.iucr.org/cgi-bin/paper?S0021889808012016">paper</a> if you use it.<br />
4. <a title="http://www.inkscape.org/" href="http://www.inkscape.org/">Inkscape.</a> Vector drawings for those Nature and PRL schematics, without the need to sell your lab coat to afford Adobe Illustrator.<br />
5. <a title="http://www.gimp.org/" href="http://www.gimp.org/">Gimp</a>. Not the leather clad man, but the bitmap manipulator for making your images publication quality, without the need to sell your colleague’s lab coat to afford Adobe Photoshop.<br />
6. <a title="http://www.acdlabs.com/products/chem_dsn_lab/chemsketch/" href="http://www.acdlabs.com/products/chem_dsn_lab/chemsketch/">Chemsketch.</a> When you need to draw some annoyingly obscure organic molecule or make use of orbital templates, the freeware Chemsketch comes in handy.</p>
